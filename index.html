The Power of Video in Today's Digital World

Video is key for engaging your audience and boosting brand awareness across platforms like Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, and Telegram.

However, producing high-quality videos can be expensive and time-consuming. Traditional production involves multiple stages, taking multiple hours and even days and costing hundreds to thousands of dollars for short videos, and even more for longer content.

Beyond production, businesses invest in compelling thumbnails and optimized descriptions. Scheduling and uploading videos also add to the complexity and cost.

The Traditional Short Video Process

Creating a short video typically involves several steps:

  1. Video Scripting and Production:
    • Plan: Defining the video idea and writing a short script.
    • Setup: Preparing the location, props, and equipment.
    • Film: Recording the video, ensuring good lighting and clear audio.
    • Edit: Using an app like InShot or CapCut to edit and add effects.
  2. Generating a Thumbnail:
    • Capture: Taking a still image from the video or designing a custom thumbnail.
    • Design: Using bold text and colors to make it visually appealing.
  3. Writing the Text for Upload:
    • Description: Writing a summary of the video with a call to action.
    • Tags: Adding relevant keywords and hashtags.
  4. Scheduling and Uploading:
    • Upload: Following platform-specific steps to upload the video.
    • Schedule: Posting at peak times using scheduling tools.
    • Promote: Sharing the video on other channels and engaging with viewers.
